Researchers at University of Virginia have received a multi-year federal research grant to study persistent broadband access gaps affecting rural communities across Virginia.

## Scope of the research

The project will combine household surveys, infrastructure mapping, and economic impact modeling to build what researchers describe as the most detailed picture yet of how connectivity gaps affect rural Virginia households and small businesses.

"Access to broadband isn't just a convenience issue anymore — it determines whether a small business can process payments, whether a student can complete homework, whether a telehealth appointment can even happen," the project's lead investigator said.

## Community involvement

The research team plans to work directly with several rural counties, recruiting local residents to participate in surveys and interviews over the next two years. Community advisory boards will help guide which questions the research prioritizes.

## Policy implications

State broadband officials say they are watching the project closely, since its findings could shape how future infrastructure funding is allocated across the Commonwealth.

"Good policy needs good data behind it," said a representative from the state's broadband office. "This kind of independent research gives us something we can actually build funding decisions around."

## Timeline

Initial findings from the first phase of data collection are expected within eighteen months, with a full report due at the conclusion of the three-year grant period.
