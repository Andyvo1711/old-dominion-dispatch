Home price growth in the Chesapeake area has slowed in recent months as mortgage rates have stabilized, giving buyers somewhat more negotiating room after several years of a tight seller's market.

## What's changing

Local real estate agents report that homes are staying on the market slightly longer than in previous years, and multiple-offer situations, while still common for well-priced properties, have become less frequent.

"It's not a buyer's market by any means, but it's not the frenzy we saw a couple of years ago either," a local real estate broker said. "Buyers finally have a little room to breathe."

## Why rates matter

Housing economists say the stabilization in mortgage rates, after a period of sharp increases, has allowed both buyers and sellers to plan with more certainty, which tends to smooth out extreme price swings.

## Impact on first-time buyers

First-time buyers in the region say the shift, while modest, has made a meaningful difference in their search.

"We lost three bidding wars last year," one recent buyer said. "This year we actually got to negotiate on price."

## What sellers are seeing

Sellers, meanwhile, report needing to be more realistic about pricing than in previous years, with homes priced aggressively above market value sitting longer before receiving offers.

## Outlook

Local economists expect the market to remain relatively balanced in the near term, barring a significant shift in interest rates or regional employment trends.
