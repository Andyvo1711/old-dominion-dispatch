University of Virginia formally opened its new Center for Applied Data Science this week, a facility administrators describe as a bridge between academic research and the state's fast-growing technology sector.

## A response to workforce demand

University leaders said the center was built in direct response to feedback from regional employers, who have reported a persistent shortage of graduates trained in statistical modeling, machine learning, and data infrastructure.

"We kept hearing the same thing from companies across the Commonwealth: they need graduates who can walk in and start working with real data on day one," said the center's founding director. "This space is designed around that exact gap."

## What the center offers

The facility includes dedicated computing labs, a data visualization studio, and project rooms where students will work directly with datasets provided by partner organizations in Arlington and beyond. The university says roughly 300 students are expected to use the space in its first year, drawn from majors as varied as computer science, public policy, and business analytics.

## Faculty and student reaction

Faculty involved in the planning process say the center will also support faculty research grants and give graduate students more structured mentorship opportunities.

"It's rare to get a space this well resourced this early in a program's life," one participating professor said. "It changes what we can realistically teach in a semester."

Students who toured the center during its opening week said they were most excited about access to industry-grade tools that are normally out of reach for undergraduates.

## Looking ahead

The university plans to expand the center's partnerships over the next two academic years, with an emphasis on public-sector data projects tied to Virginia state agencies.
