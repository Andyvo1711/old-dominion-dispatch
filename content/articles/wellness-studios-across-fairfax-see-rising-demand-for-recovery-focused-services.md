Wellness studios across Fairfax report a notable rise in client demand for recovery-focused services, including cold plunge therapy, compression treatments, and infrared sauna sessions, over the past year.

## A shift in what clients want

Studio owners say the trend reflects a broader shift in how clients think about wellness, moving beyond traditional spa treatments toward services marketed around physical recovery and stress management.

"Five years ago almost nobody asked about cold plunge," one studio owner said. "Now it's one of the first questions new clients ask about."

## Who's driving demand

While recovery-focused services were once associated primarily with athletes, studio owners say the client base has broadened significantly to include office workers, parents, and older adults managing chronic stress or minor pain.

"It's not just triathletes anymore," a wellness studio manager said. "We have retirees who come in twice a week just to feel better in their joints."

## Investment in new equipment

Several Fairfax-area studios have invested in new equipment over the past year to keep up with demand, including dedicated cold plunge tubs and additional infrared sauna units.

## What clients are saying

Regular clients describe the recovery-focused sessions as a meaningful addition to their routines.

"I was skeptical at first, but the difference in how I sleep on the nights I do a session is noticeable," one client said.

## Looking ahead

Industry observers expect demand for recovery-focused wellness services to continue growing in the region as awareness spreads.
