New lending data released this week shows small business borrowing in the Norfolk region has climbed compared to the same period last year, a trend regional bankers attribute to improving borrower confidence.

## What the data shows

Community banks and credit unions serving the Norfolk area reported an increase in small business loan originations, with growth concentrated in sectors like retail, professional services, and light manufacturing.

"We're seeing business owners who spent the last couple of years being cautious finally start to invest again," said a regional bank's commercial lending director. "That shows up directly in loan applications."

## What's driving the change

Bankers point to a combination of factors, including easing inflation pressures on input costs and steadier consumer demand, as reasons business owners feel more comfortable taking on debt to expand.

## A note of caution

Not all lenders are equally optimistic. Some credit union officials caution that loan demand remains uneven across sectors, with hospitality and retail recovering more slowly than professional services.

"It's not a uniform recovery," one credit union executive said. "Some of our members are thriving, and others are still just holding steady."

## What business owners are saying

Local business owners who recently secured financing say the improved lending environment has allowed them to move forward with expansion plans they had shelved during leaner years.

## Looking ahead

Economists tracking the region say continued stability in interest rates will likely be the biggest factor determining whether the lending uptick continues into next year.
